H2: Why Cabinet Painting Fails

Most cabinet paint jobs fail because they are done incorrectly.

Common mistakes:
• Skipping proper degreasing
• Inadequate sanding or surface preparation
• Incorrect or missing primer
• Brush or roller application
• Low-quality paint products

This leads to:
• Peeling finishes
• Chipping edges
• Uneven texture
• Sticky or soft surfaces

👉 Cabinets require a completely different system than walls — shortcuts will fail.

H2: Our Cabinet Refinishing Process

We follow a structured system designed for long-term performance:

Removal
• Doors, drawers, and hardware are removed

Cleaning
• Deep degreasing to eliminate oils and buildup

Surface Preparation
• Sanding before primer
• Smoothing and correcting imperfections

Priming
• Bonding primer for maximum adhesion

Sanding (After Primer)
• Light sanding for a smooth, even base

Application
• Professional spray finish
• No brush marks, no roller texture

Curing
• Controlled drying process for durability

Reinstallation
• Doors and hardware reinstalled cleanly

👉 The process is what creates the result — not just the paint.

H2: Spray Finish vs Brush & Roller

There is a clear difference between basic cabinet painting and professional refinishing.

Brush/Roller Finish:
• Visible texture
• Uneven surface
• Lower durability

Spray Finish (Our System):
• Smooth, factory-like finish
• Even, consistent coverage
• Higher durability

👉 If cabinets are not sprayed, they will not achieve a true high-end result.
